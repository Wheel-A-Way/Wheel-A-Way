EscapeVRoom Devlog

Weekly development updates for our VR escape room project

3/29/2026

This week, our group focused on continuing development of our final project, EscapeVRoom, a VR escape-room game set in a laser tag building where players navigate the environment as a wheelchair user. The goal of the project is to create an immersive experience that helps non-disabled players better understand the frustration and difficulty that inaccessible spaces can create for people with mobility disabilities.

This week, we made progress on building the core structure of the game. We completed the layout of all five rooms, which gives us the overall spatial framework of the experience and helps define how players will move through the environment. Since the setting is a laser tag facility, we wanted the rooms to feel maze-like and immersive while also reinforcing the project’s focus on accessibility barriers and constrained navigation.

We also developed the puzzle for the tutorial room. In this puzzle, the player must search for a grabber arm in order to retrieve a key that is placed out of reach on a bookshelf. This introduces the player to the game’s core idea right away by showing how something simple, like reaching an object, can become a challenge when the environment is not designed with accessibility in mind.

In addition, we completed the pawn model for the player. This is an important step because it provides the basis for player movement, interaction, and embodiment in the VR experience. Since EscapeVRoom depends heavily on immersion and perspective, having the pawn model in place is a key part of building the overall experience.

Overall, this week’s progress gave us the full room layout for the game, an introductory puzzle that reflects the project’s central theme, and the player pawn model that will support future interaction and movement systems. Our next steps are to continue implementing puzzles in the remaining rooms and refining the gameplay experience so that it is both engaging and meaningful.
